## Overview

<AddedIn version="4.2.6" />

Color and background helpers combine the power of our [`.text-*` utilities](/utilities/colors/) and [`.bg-*` utilities](/utilities/background/) in one class. Using our Sass `color-contrast()` function, we automatically determine a contrasting `color` for a particular `background-color`.

<Callout color="warning">
**Heads up!** There's currently no support for a CSS-native `color-contrast` function, so we use our own via Sass. This means that customizing our theme colors via CSS variables may cause color contrast issues with these utilities.
</Callout>

<Example lang="html" name="helpers-color-background-1" componentName="Color & background" code={`<div class="text-bg-primary p-3">Primary with contrasting color</div>
  <div class="text-bg-secondary p-3">Secondary with contrasting color</div>
  <div class="text-bg-success p-3">Success with contrasting color</div>
  <div class="text-bg-danger p-3">Danger with contrasting color</div>
  <div class="text-bg-warning p-3">Warning with contrasting color</div>
  <div class="text-bg-info p-3">Info with contrasting color</div>`} />

## With components

Use them in place of combined `.text-*` and `.bg-*` classes, like on [badges](/components/badge/#background-colors):

<Example lang="html" name="helpers-color-background-2" componentName="Color & background" code={`<span class="badge text-bg-primary">Primary</span>
  <span class="badge text-bg-info">Info</span>`} />

Or on [cards](/components/card/#background-and-color):

<Example lang="html" name="helpers-color-background-3" componentName="Color & background" code={`<div class="card text-bg-primary mb-3" style="max-width: 18rem;">
    <div class="card-header">Header</div>
    <div class="card-body">
      <p class="card-text">Some quick example text to build on the card title and make up the bulk of the card's content.</p>
    </div>
  </div>
  <div class="card text-bg-info mb-3" style="max-width: 18rem;">
    <div class="card-header">Header</div>
    <div class="card-body">
      <p class="card-text">Some quick example text to build on the card title and make up the bulk of the card's content.</p>
    </div>
  </div>`} />
